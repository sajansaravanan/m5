Carina Nebula

The Carina Nebula is one of the largest and brightest nebulae in the sky, located about 7,500 light-years from Earth.

It is a stunning region of star formation, filled with dense clouds of gas and dust.

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope has captured detailed images of this nebula, revealing its beautiful structure.

The nebula hosts some of the most massive stars known, making it a key area for understanding stellar evolution.

The Carina Nebula continues to be a subject of fascination for astronomers and space enthusiasts alike.
